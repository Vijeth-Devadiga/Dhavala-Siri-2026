About the Fest

Dhavala Siri 2025–26

A vibrant platform for artistic abilities, intellectual skills, and innovative ideas among Mangalore University students.

Dhavala Siri 2025–26 is a vibrant cultural and creative fest that celebrates talent, creativity, and collaboration among college students. Organised by Sri Dhavala College, the fest brings together students from degree and postgraduate colleges affiliated with Mangalore University.

The festival provides a platform for students to showcase their artistic abilities, intellectual skills, and innovative ideas through multiple events including cultural performances, quiz competitions, rangoli art, and social media creativity challenges. Each event encourages originality, teamwork, and creative expression.

Participants compete in both individual and group categories, presenting performances such as dance, mono acting, mime, mimicry, and thematic skits. The fest also features intellectually stimulating quiz contests, traditional rangoli design competitions, and a modern Instagram Reels challenge.

Dhavala Siri is not just a competition — it is a celebration of culture, talent, and youthful energy. Winners are recognised with cash prizes, trophies, and special awards for outstanding performances.

Through this celebration of talent and culture, Dhavala Siri aims to inspire creativity, promote cultural appreciation, and provide a memorable experience for students from across the region.

About the Institution

Sri Dhavala College

Established 1965 · Moodabidri, Karnataka · Mangalore University Affiliated

Sri Dhavala College was established in 1965 by the Sri Dhavala Education Trust with the vision of providing accessible and quality education to students from rural and disadvantaged communities in and around Moodabidri. Since its inception, the institution has remained committed to empowering young minds through education, values, and disciplined learning.

The college was founded to create opportunities for students who may not otherwise have access to higher education. Along with academic excellence, the institution places strong emphasis on character development, moral values, and discipline, helping students grow into responsible and confident individuals.

Over the decades, Sri Dhavala College has evolved into one of the leading pre-university institutions in the state, widely recognised for consistent academic achievements, dedicated faculty, and a supportive learning environment.

With a legacy spanning several decades, the college continues to uphold its mission of delivering quality education while nurturing talent, integrity, and leadership among its students.

Competition Guidelines

General Rules

Rules applicable to all participants and teams across all events. Additional event-specific rules are available on the Events page.

01
No Entry Fee — There is no entry fee for any competition at Dhavala Siri.
02
Eligibility — Participation is limited only to Degree and PG Colleges affiliated with Mangalore University.
03
One Team Per College — Only one team per college is allowed to participate in the cultural performance event.
04
Team Size — Minimum 6 members and maximum 16 members for cultural events.
05
Theme Compliance — All performances must be based on the main theme of Dhavala Siri.
06
Time Limit — 20 minutes for performance + 5 min setup + 5 min clear. Performances exceeding time will not be evaluated.
07
Cultural Sensitivity — Performances must not disrespect religion, caste, or cultural practices of Tulu Nadu.
08
Evaluation — Judges assess Creativity, Activity, Presentation, and Costumes.
09
Technical Media — CD or pen drive allowed; live performance also permitted. Test media before the event. Organisers not responsible for technical issues.
10
Registration Deadline — Teams must send names via Email or WhatsApp on or before 01-04-2026.
11
Facilities — Only basic lighting and microphone facilities will be provided.
12
Meals — Participants will be provided breakfast and lunch on the event day.
13
Identification — Carry your College ID Card and a confirmation letter from the Principal.
14
Judges' Decision — The decision of the judges is final and binding across all events.
